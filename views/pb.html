Popular Business Trends of the Next 10 Years

Updated February 3, 2023

Keeping updated with the latest business trends can help organizations plan for the future and implement exciting innovations to increase profitability and customer satisfaction. These can help businesses identify what customers might want or what tools they can use in the future so they can adjust their strategies to meet their goals. Learning about these trends can help you discover why understanding them can be crucial for an organization's success.

In this article, we discuss what business trends are, the types of trends that can affect businesses and some specific trends you might encounter in different industries.

What are business trends?

Business trends are new developments in a market or industry that affect the business environment. They're typically new ways that businesses hope to achieve their goals or improve their performance among their competitors. Trends include what products you can offer, how manufacturing and other processes operate, how you can gain funding and how you can connect with customers. Often, one or a few companies might start with a new strategy or product, and others might follow if it becomes successful, starting a trend.

Related: How To Stay Current With Technology Trends


Types of business trends

There are several different types of business trends that are important in the current environment, although many trends can fit into several categories. For example, cryptocurrency is both new technology and a financial trend. Some examples of types of trends include the following:

  • Technological innovation continues to inspire significant developments, especially in communications and data analysis.

  • Financial innovation is also important as businesses continue to find new ways to raise capital, fund expansion and charge customers for their products.

  • Small business innovation is significant as smaller businesses are often more agile and adaptable than larger businesses.

  • Behavioral trends rely on customer and employee data, as the demographics of the country change and people respond to broader trends in society.

10 common business trends

Here's a list of business trends you might see in the next several years:


1. Artificial intelligence

Technological progress in this area is rapid, which allows for nearly instantaneous processing of vast amounts of data. This is impactful upon all areas of business, from more efficient warehousing and manufacturing practices to offering personalized user experiences to the end customer. Companies that leverage artificial intelligence most effectively, using the latest developments in areas such as machine learning and deep learning, can increase productivity and profitability.

Related: Deep Learning vs. Machine Learning: Learn the Difference


2. Alternative sources of finance

There are now many options on how to finance your business and process payments, especially for small businesses. Blockchain technology and cryptocurrencies might become a more important part of the financial landscape, while crowdfunding is a significant part of how many small businesses gain financial backing for new products and services. For consumers, there are innovative new practices to consider. For example, subscription-based pricing is increasingly popular for all kinds of products, from video streaming to razor blades. Exploring all the different options for business finance and consumer pricing can help your business build profits and grow sustainably.


3. Augmented reality and virtual reality

Augmented and virtual reality technologies are relatively new, and the possibilities for new and enhanced applications are plentiful. Augmented and virtual reality technology can be useful for marketing and communications methods, as a training tool or a form of therapy and to enhance accessibility. For example, furniture stores can allow people to view how a new sofa might fit into their living room before purchasing it.


4. New generations

Millennials are well-established in the workforce, many of whom hold important decision-making positions. They're also significant as consumers, with their preferences guiding many trends in consumption patterns. Members of Generation Z are now in the workforce and are an important consumer segment. These generations have some big behavior differences from older generations, such as how comfortable they feel with digital technologies. Those companies that can both market successfully to these generations and attract and retain them as employees can gain significant benefits now and in the future.


5. Environmentalism

Consumers might increase their interest in the environmental impact of products and services when making purchasing decisions. Reducing the environmental cost of your company's products can represent proper business acumen and can even form a crucial component of your marketing strategy. Small businesses have an advantage in this situation as they can integrate environmentally friendly practices more easily and ensure that business growth is environmentally sustainable.

Related: Sustainable Strategy: The Benefits and How To Create One for Your Business


6. Targeted social media marketing

Social media is now well-established as a marketing platform, but there are also new developments. Viral marketing can be more successful than paid promotions, while targeting specific groups via micro-influencers can be more effective than trying to reach as many people as possible. Live video can be a particularly effective tool to drive engagement. Businesses that use social media marketing in a carefully targeted manner can reach their customers much more efficiently, especially younger generations who consume much less traditional media than their parents.

Read more: What Is Social Media Marketing?


7. Remote working and remote learning

Many employees work remotely and complete training online. Remote working offers benefits, and companies that consider their employees' desires for flexibility generally have better employee satisfaction than those with more rigid working practices. However, remote working can also lead to less team cohesion, so it's important to provide the benefits of in-person interaction to the virtual workplace. Networks that encourage informal discussion can be effective. It's also worth considering occasional in-person events and then following these up with digital communications to sustain the benefits of networking long term.


8. E-commerce

Similar to remote work and education, customers may purchase more items online with e-commerce practices. Rather than finding retail locations in person, the option to shop online can provide more opportunities for customers to purchase products. There are often trends related to e-commerce, like easy return options or saving customer information for easy checkout.


9. Agility

Many companies operate with traditional hierarchy structures that may be difficult to change over time. With different project management tools and technologies, companies may look to become more agile with their operations and offerings. A less rigid hierarchy could encourage lower-level employees to suggest changes and help company leadership learn more about the actions needed to fulfill business needs.


10. Value through purpose

People might find companies that align with their values more appealing when shopping. As a trend, companies might show more of what they value through partnerships or community engagement. By doing this, organizations can reach new customers and show their loyal customers their commitment to their shared mission statements and how they might contribute to a more positive world.
